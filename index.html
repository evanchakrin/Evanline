How to Use

Modes

Camber

Place the phone flush against the wheel face (hub area), screen facing outward. The angle shown is the camber — tilt relative to vertical. Negative = wheels lean inward (negative camber), positive = lean outward.

Toe

Place the phone against the inside face of the wheel, pointing forward. Rotate until one edge touches the wheel flat. The angle approximates toe-in/toe-out relative to your calibrated zero.

Level

Place the phone on a flat reference surface (roof, sill, hub). This shows left-right tilt — useful for ensuring your car is on level ground before aligning.

Pitch

Shows front-to-back pitch angle of the phone. Useful for measuring surface gradient under the car or checking caster-related tilt.

Step-by-Step

1
Park on level ground Use the Level mode to verify the surface is as flat as possible before any alignment measurements.
2
Calibrate on a known surface Hold the phone on a true-level surface or in a known orientation, then tap Zero / Calibrate to set the reference to zero.
3
Measure each wheel In Camber mode, hold the phone flat against the wheel face with screen outward. Read the angle. Typical spec: −0.5° to −1.5° for most vehicles.
4
Use Lock to hold a reading Tap the ⏸ button to freeze the current reading so you can note it down.
5
Compare and adjust Compare front-left vs front-right (or rear-left vs rear-right). Adjust tie rods (toe) or control arm bolts (camber) as appropriate. Recheck each time.

Important Notes

!
Consumer-grade accuracy Phone sensors are consumer-grade (±0.1°–0.5° typical). Results are indicative and suitable for DIY reference. Professional alignment requires a calibrated laser rack.
!
Safari on iOS required Motion sensors require Safari on iPhone (iOS 13+). You must tap "Enable Sensors" and grant permission in the dialog that appears.
