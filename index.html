Geometric toe wizard
Measure toe geometrically

A phone cannot sense toe. Enter measured rim/plate gaps and the app is the calculator + uncertainty engine. Toe-in is positive. Target accuracy ~0.1–0.3°.

Why not phone-only sensor toe? Toe is a yaw (rotation about vertical) measurement. A phone's yaw drifts without a magnetometer reference, and a magnetometer is unreliable next to a steel body. iOS Safari also has no ARKit/WebXR for drift-free tracking. So Evanline measures toe geometrically instead — the method below is accurate and repeatable.
Runout is the dominant error. Roll the wheel ~180°, re-seat, and measure twice. The app refuses a single read and averages the two so rim runout cancels. Reference the rim, never the tire sidewall or lettering.

Precision · string-box per-wheel + thrust

Pull two strings parallel to the car (one down each side, equal stand-off front and rear). For each wheel, measure the gap from the string to the rim at the FRONT edge and the REAR edge over span D. This splits L vs R, gives total front/rear toe, and the THRUST angle — no symmetry assumption.

Do not use the phone compass to square the box near the car body — indoor steel and the body distort the heading. Square the box geometrically (equal diagonals / 3-4-5), never off the magnetometer.
Box-squaring checklist
  • Set both strings the SAME stand-off from the hub centers, front and rear.
  • Square the box with equal diagonals or a 3-4-5 triangle — not the compass.
  • Reference the RIM at a repeatable radius, never the tire sidewall or lettering.
  • Roll the car forward and re-seat before reading to settle suspension and cancel runout.
  • Keep the string height level so front/rear gaps are read at the same point.

Caster (camber swing)
Measure caster from a camber swing

Caster is not a static reading — it is recovered from how camber changes as the wheel is steered. Hold the phone flush to the wheel face (same as camber), steer a known angle each way, and capture camber at both positions. caster = (camberout − camberin) / (2 · sin θ). Honest accuracy ~±0.5–1°.

No zeroing needed. Caster is a DIFFERENCE of two camber reads, so any camber zero offset cancels — capture RAW camber at both steer positions. Use turn plates with degree markings (or a printable degree disc) to set the steer angle each way.

How to Use

Modes

Camber

Quick mode still works for fast checks. For best repeatability, switch to Precision, use a rigid jig, capture a Level baseline around the car, then take repeated forward and reversed readings before saving camber.

Toe (geometric)

The phone cannot sense toe — a gravity sensor is blind to rotation about the vertical axis and there is no vehicle-centerline reference. So the Toe tab is a guided geometric calculator, not a sensor read. Quick path: plates + 2 tapes give TOTAL axle toe = atan((rear gap R − front gap F) / measured span D); enter a measured reference span (not a nominal lookup), then do the forced roll-and-average runout step — roll the wheel ~180°, re-seat, and measure again so rim runout cancels (the app refuses a single read). Always reference the rim, not the tire sidewall or lettering. Precision path: a string box splits left vs right per wheel and reports total front/rear toe plus the thrust angle; square the box geometrically (equal diagonals / 3-4-5), never off the phone compass. Every result carries a ±band and the linear equivalent at the diameter it assumes. Honest accuracy is ~0.2–0.3°.

Caster (camber swing)

Caster is not a static gravity reading — a phone held still cannot sense it. Instead it is recovered from how camber changes as the wheel is steered. Hold the phone flush to the wheel face (the same camber pose), steer a known angle θ each way (use turn plates with degree markings or a printable degree disc; classic value 20°, total swing = 2θ), and capture a settled camber read at each position. caster = (camberout − camberin) / (2 · sin θ). Because it is a difference of two camber reads, any camber zero offset cancels — there is no zeroing step; capture raw camber both ways. Each read's ±band is propagated (≈2× the per-read camber band at 20°). It is a front-wheel measurement (FL/FR). The magnitude is robust, but the absolute sign must be validated against a known-caster reference (positive caster = top of the steering axis tilted rearward). Honest accuracy is ~±0.5–1°.

Level

Precision mode uses Level to capture FL, FR, RL, and RR baseline points. This turns one quick level check into a session-wide reference plane for later trust scoring.

Pitch

Pitch remains available, but the precision workflow prioritizes repeatable camber, toe, and baseline setup first. Use it to spot front/rear attitude changes inside the same session.

Physical placement

C
Camber — register to the wheel FACE Hold the phone flat against the machined wheel face, clear of the curved outer lip and any decorative or raised spokes. Wheel-face camber is not the same as tire camber — register to the same hard, flat datum on every corner so left/right deltas are comparable.
K
Caster — camber pose, two steer positions Hold the phone on the wheel face exactly as for camber, but capture it twice: once with the wheel steered out by θ and once steered in by θ. Set θ with turn plates or a printable degree disc (default 20° each way). No zeroing — the camber offset cancels in the difference. Let each read settle before capturing. Front wheels only; validate the absolute sign against a known-caster reference.
L
Level & Pitch — use a fixed body datum Place the phone flat on a repeatable, rigid body datum (a flat sill, a hub face, the same roof spot, or a chassis rail) — not on flexible trim. Re-use the exact same surface for every reading so the plane reference stays consistent.
T
Toe — geometric method only Do not read toe from phone tilt. Use the guided Toe wizard: enter the front-vs-rear rim/plate gap over a measured reference span D (the app computes atan(offset / D)). Roll the wheel ~180° and re-measure so runout averages out, and reference the rim, never the tire sidewall or lettering. The precision string-box path adds per-wheel split and thrust angle.

Step-by-Step

1
Start a precision session Switch from Quick to Precision workflow when you want the app to track a device reference, fixture profile, baseline points, and repeated capture sets.
2
Calibrate the hardware stack Capture a trusted device reference, save or select a named fixture profile, and prefer a reversible 3-point jig that lands on the same wheel datum every time.
3
Measure baseline points around the car Use Level mode to capture FL, FR, RL, and RR. The app uses those points to report whether your baseline plane looks trusted, approximate, or too noisy.
4
Capture forward and reversed sets For each side, take repeated settled readings in the normal fixture orientation, then flip the jig or phone in the reversible direction and capture a reversed set to expose mounting bias.
5
Save only when the report looks trustworthy Precision saves now include repeatability, reversal bias, baseline quality, capture count, and a verdict so you can decide whether the number is good enough for comparison or adjustment.

Important Notes

!
3D-printed jig expectations Aim for rigid 3-point wheel-face contact, avoid tire sidewalls, make the jig symmetric or reversible, and add hard phone registration so each re-seat lands in the same place.
!
Consumer-grade sensors still need real-world validation Phone sensors can be very repeatable with a disciplined workflow, but you should still benchmark your setup against a digital angle gauge or professional rack before trusting adjustment values.
!
Safari on iPhone required Motion sensors require Safari on iPhone (iOS 13+). You must tap “Enable Sensors & Start” and approve the permission prompt.
