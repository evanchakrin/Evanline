How to Use

Modes

Camber

Place the phone flush against the wheel face with the screen facing outward. Use portrait orientation, zero against a known reference, then wait for the reading to settle before saving each side.

Toe

Use a straight edge or known straight-ahead position as your zero reference first. Then measure each side the same way and compare the saved averages instead of chasing live jitter.

Level

Use this first to verify the car or the floor is level. Landscape orientation is preferred for a wider bubble reference and more obvious left-right tilt.

Pitch

Place the phone front-to-back on a flat surface under the car. Landscape orientation helps keep the reading consistent while you wait for the sensor to settle.

Step-by-Step

1
Verify the surface Start in Level mode and make sure the car or floor is close to level before adjusting alignment settings.
2
Zero the current mode Each mode keeps its own calibration. Use Zero This Mode on a known reference so you do not lose your other zero points.
3
Match the preferred orientation Camber and Toe prefer portrait. Level and Pitch prefer landscape. Rotate the phone until the orientation chip shows a match.
4
Wait for Settled The app smooths the sensor feed and watches recent sample spread. Save readings only after stability turns to Settled.
5
Save and compare sides Pick FL, FR, RL, or RR, then tap Save Avg. Evanline stores readings locally and shows front/rear deltas for the active mode.

Important Notes

!
Consumer-grade accuracy Phone sensors typically land around ±0.1°–0.5°. Averaging and settled detection improve repeatability, but a calibrated laser rack is still the professional standard.
!
Safari on iPhone required Motion sensors require Safari on iPhone (iOS 13+). You must tap “Enable Sensors & Start” and approve the permission prompt.
